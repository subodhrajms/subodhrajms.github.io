About

I am a Senior Researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), Gj√∏vik, where I work on biometric recognition systems with a primary focus on iris and face recognition. My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning, and signal processing, with particular emphasis on quality-aware and robust vision systems, explainable AI, and attention- and graph-based learning. I hold a PhD in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the National Institute of Technology Calicut, where my doctoral work, "From Recovery to Recognition: Analysis of Human Poses, Actions, and Activities," addressed human pose, action, and activity analysis using low-rank and tensor modeling, subspace learning, and deep learning frameworks.

Alongside my research career, I have extensive experience in teaching, mentoring, and applied engineering. I have served as an Assistant Professor in multiple institutions, delivering undergraduate and postgraduate courses in electronics, embedded systems, and machine learning, supervising student projects, and contributing to academic administration. I have also worked in industry as an AI/ML Computational Science Specialist, developing scalable, cloud-native AI backends and production-ready machine learning systems. Through my work in academia, industry, and international research collaborations, I aim to develop reliable, interpretable, and deployable intelligent vision systems grounded in strong theoretical foundations and real-world impact.
