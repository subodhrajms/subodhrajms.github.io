About

I am a Computer Vision/ Machine Learning enthusiast currently pursuing a Ph.D. in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at the National Institute of Technology, Calicut, India. My current research endeavors are focused on the development of innovative computer vision techniques for the analysis of human actions. In particular, my research involves the design and development of unsupervised optimization models and supervised neural network architectures to enable the recovery of human actions from corrupted motion capture sequences, the recognition of human activities from motion capture sequences, and the recognition of human poses from depth maps.

Apart from this, during my tenure as a research scholar, I have worked on interesting projects, such as moving object detection from video sequences and hand gesture recognition for home automation and related applications. Before starting my Ph.D. journey, I worked as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering, at the Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology, Thrissur, India, affiliated with the APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University, Kerala (formerly Kerala Technological University). I hold an M.Tech degree in Electronics Design Technology from the National Institute of Electronics and Information Technology, Calicut (affiliated with the University of Calicut), and a B.Tech degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from AWH Engineering College, Calicut (affiliated with the University of Calicut).
